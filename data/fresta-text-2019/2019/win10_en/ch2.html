Before starting this chapter

In this chapter, we will describe your works assuming that you have a USB stick that was handed in the workshop.

In the case that you did not take the workshop, please choose from following two options:

  1. Borrowing a USB stick at Information Media Center Counter, and following the instructions on this chapter.
  2. Downloading programs, the contents of USB stick, from Web site directly, installing them, and proceeding to the next chapter.

    * Please follow the instructions on the following sites. If you are asked ID and password, please input HIRODAI ID (student ID) and HIRODAI password (printed on a paper handed with your ID card)

    • Office
    • Chrome（Web browser）

Copy the files in the USB flash drive to your PC

[1] Insert the USB flash drive into a USB port on your PC. If your PC recognizes the flash drive, it will make a sound.

[2] When the message about FIRST LEC appears on the lower right of the desktop, click it.

[3] Choose "Open folder to view files" in "FIRST LEC (D:)" window.

[4] When "FIRST LEC (D:)" window appears, drag and drop "setup2019-win10en" folder to your desktop.

* Here is other way to copy the folder; choose it, press "Ctrl" key and c, click the desktop, and press "Ctrl" key and v.

[5] When you have finished the copy, you need to eject the USB flash drive from your PC. Right-click "FIRST LEC (D:)" on the left side of "FIRST LEC (D:)" window and click "Eject" in the list.

Install Microsoft Office

If Office2016 has already been installed on your PC, you can skip this section and proceed to step [10]. If you have Office2013 in your PC, you may install Office2016 on your PC.

[6] Double-click the copied "setup2019-win10en" folder on your desktop to open it. Then double-click the "office2016win-en" in the "windows-en" window. When you find the "install" item in the folder, double-click to start the installation.

[7] In order to install software, the installation program needs to create files in the area which PC user may not access for the general use. Therefore, OS asks you if you really allow the installation. Here, click "Yes".

[8] The window says, "We'll be done in just a moment." But...

[9] When you see the window in the picture, the installation has been completed. Click "Close" to proceed the next step.

Install Google Chrome

[10] Double-click "ChromeStandaloneSetup64" in the copied "windows-en" folder on your desktop. When you are asked to allow the app to make changes to your PC, click "Yes".

[11] The installation will be completed in a minute!

* You may realize that Microsoft Edge, as the default browser, has already been in Windows 10. But there are some web pages which cannot be displayed successfully by Microsoft Edge. In that case, you can use Google Chrome as the secondary browser.
